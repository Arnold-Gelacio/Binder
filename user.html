01 — Short-Form / FYP

Tiktok
Warmup

A 3-day protocol for a brand-new account, before the first real post goes up.

Applies to TikTok. This platforms treat a new account as a likely bot until it proves otherwise — skip the warmup and the usual result is a shadowban or the well-known "200-view jail."

Setup, before Day 1

  • Complete the profile 100%: a real profile photo (not stock or a meme), a filled-out bio, a username relevant to the niche
  • Enable two-factor authentication, use a valid email
  • No link in the bio yet
  • Post from the mobile app only for the first 10+ posts — no web uploads, no scheduling tools, no API
Auto-flags to avoid
  • Posting within 24 hours of account creation
  • A link in the bio on day one
  • Mass follow / unfollow
  • Engaging in only one narrow niche — mix in adjacent content so the pattern reads as human
  • Posting multiple times before any engagement comes in
  • Following everyone who follows back
  • Comment strings that are just emojis — real words only

Day 1 — Pure scroll

  • 30+ minutes on the FYP (TikTok)
  • Watch videos all the way through — no skipping in a repetitive pattern
  • Like 15–20 videos in the niche, spread out rather than in rapid succession
  • Leave 3-5 genuine comments — real words, not emojis
  • Follow 1-2 accounts in the niche
  • Save 2–3 videos actually worth saving, as a signal of real interest
  • No posting. No links.

Day 2 — Train the algorithm

  • Repeat the Day 1 engagement pattern
  • Search niche keywords directly in the search bar
  • Watch 10+ niche videos all the way through
  • Still no posting, still no links

Day 3 — Drop one warmup post

  • Not promotional — niche content only
  • A trending sound from the last 7 days (TikTok)
  • Unique footage — no duplicates, no recycled clips
  • You should see a niche video on your first feed, if not continue day 2
  • Post from the mobile app
  • Wait 24 hours and watch how the views land
The test

1K+ views on that first post means the account is cleared to post freely.

After warmup

  • Keep 5–10 minutes of daily niche engagement going — likes, comments, watches
  • The first 10 posts should still go through the mobile app, not web or a scheduler
  • Add the bio link only once views are landing consistently
02 — Short-Form / Reels

Instagram Warmup

A nine-phase system for creating, warming, and posting an Instagram Reels account aimed at a US audience.

Phase 1 — Account creation

Email setup: use a fresh Gmail or Outlook account. Don't create accounts in bulk — a maximum of two on the first day, then a two-day wait before creating another, repeating that pacing going forward.

Initial setup: set the name, username, and profile picture immediately during creation; leave the bio empty for now.

The five-minute rule: leave the account completely idle for five minutes right after creation — no interactions, no edits.

Phase 2 — Initial warm-up (algorithm training)

After the five minutes, start watching Reels and engaging naturally — avoid spam-liking.

Keyword targeting — search USA-based niche terms directly: Polymarket, trading, wealth, make money, clipping.

Content filtering: watch only English-language content, and immediately mark irrelevant or local content as "Not Interested" so the feed converges toward roughly 90% niche-related, USA/English content.

Duration: 20–30 minutes total; one day is enough for this phase. Like, save, and interact naturally throughout.

Phase 3 — Professional optimization

Switch the account to Creator/Professional. Complete every dashboard checkmark, and add a bio and links if needed — but don't keep revising bio or settings; set it once or twice, maximum.

Deep warm-up: keep liking, saving, and following niche accounts, spreading the actions across time rather than rushing them.

Phase 4 — Technical setup

  • Resolution: 1080p
  • Frame rate: 30 FPS strictly — avoid 60 FPS on Instagram
  • Enable "Upload at Highest Quality"
  • Enable "Translate Reel"

Phase 5 — Audio optimization

Use a song from Instagram's own music library, keeping original audio at 100% and layering background music low underneath. The logic: Instagram prioritizes content that uses its own native features.

Phase 6 — Caption strategy

Option A leans on AI-generated captions — detailed, keyword-rich, and context-based — for authority-building and stable reach. The idea is that a long, specific, on-topic caption acts as metadata: it helps the platform read content context, audience targeting, and distribution signals, which in turn helps push a well-edited clip further.

Strict guideline

Avoid spam-style captions — irrelevant keyword stuffing or generic viral formats unrelated to the actual post. These read as low-quality or spam signals rather than genuine metadata.

Phase 7 — Audience targeting

Set a minimum age filter on traffic from lower-CPM regions, with the intent of filtering out low-value traffic and pushing distribution toward a higher-value US audience.

Phase 8 — Posting strategy

Post after 1:30 PM EDT (1:30 AM PHT)to land inside US peak hours. Run five reels per session, with a 10-minute gap between each upload.

Phase 9 — Scaling logic

Expect roughly 2K–5K views per reel within the first 24 hours on a new account. If an account clears 20K+ views within a week, scale it further; if it doesn't, rotate to a fresh account instead.

Execution checklist

  • Feed is ~90% USA niche content
  • Video specs: 1080p, 30 FPS
  • Highest-quality upload enabled
  • IG music added, audio balanced
  • Caption applied (AI SEO style)
  • Minimum age filter set
  • Posting after 1:30 PM EDT - 1:30 PHT
  • Five reels uploaded, 10-minute gaps
03 — YouTube

The YouTube Growth Blueprint

2026 creator guide — how the algorithm actually tests a video, and how to set a channel up around that.

How the algorithm works now

Subscriber count matters less than the reaction a video gets right now. On upload, a small preview audience sees it first (a CTR test); if they click and stay to watch (a retention test), it gets pushed to a progressively larger audience. A brand-new channel has the same shot at going viral overnight as an established one.

Upload → small preview group (CTR test) → viewers stay (retention test) → wider push

Channel setup — the first 72 hours

Warning

Don't upload a batch of videos on day one — it reads as a spam account and can flag distribution before the channel even gets going.

Spend the first three days using the account like a normal viewer: 30–60 minutes a day watching videos in the target niche, liking and leaving real comments, and building a couple of clean public playlists around the topic. This teaches the platform what the channel is about before it has to guess from a first upload.

Day 1 — Pure scroll

  • Do not make a channel, just scroll
  • 30+ minutes on Shorts
  • Watch niche videos all the way through — no skipping in a repetitive pattern
  • Like 15–20 videos in the niche, spread out rather than in rapid succession
  • Follow 1-2 accounts in the niche
  • Save 2–3 videos actually worth saving, as a signal of real interest
  • No posting. No links.

Day 2 — Train the algorithm

  • Repeat the Day 1 engagement pattern
  • Search niche keywords directly in the search bar
  • Watch 10+ niche videos all the way through
  • Still no posting, still no links

Day 3 — Channel Creation

  • Scroll for 20m then create a channel
  • Setup name and bio
  • Scroll niche videos for 30m
  • Search niche keywords
  • Verify number for trust score
  • No posting, input link if needed

Day 4 — Drop one warmup post

  • Not promotional — niche content only
  • You should see a niche video on your first feed, if not continue scrolling on niche video
  • Open Youtube Studio to check analytics
  • Post from the mobile app
  • Wait 24 hours and watch how the views land

Profile — the storefront

ElementThe ruleWhy

Shorts — getting discovered fast

  • One niche only — mixing topics confuses the recommendation system about who to show the channel to
  • 15–35 seconds — short enough that most people watch to the end
  • Avoid copyrighted music that is longer than 60s - Auto Restrict
  • The 2-second hook — no intro, no logo animation, no "hey guys" — open on the actual hook line

Editing mechanics

  1. Cut the silence — remove every pause and slow transition, keep the audio continuous
  2. Change the screen every 2 seconds — a zoom, a bold on-screen word, or a visual change to hold attention
  3. The infinite loop trick — write the last line so it flows back into the first, so people rewatch without noticing

Long-form — building a real audience

Center long videos around a concrete challenge or goal rather than passive gameplay or talking — a defined stake gives people a reason to stay to the end. Sweet spot for length: 8–16 minutes. The intended funnel: Shorts bring in new eyes fast, long-form turns those casual viewers into a loyal audience.

Packaging — titles and thumbnails

Thumbnail: 2–3 elements max, high contrast, legible in a single glance while scrolling.

Title: skip sequential episode numbers in favor of a question or unresolved mystery, and don't give away the answer in the title — the payoff needs to stay inside the video.

Upload schedule

  • Shorts: 2–5 a day, spaced at least 3 hours apart
  • Long-form: 1–3 a week
  • Target upload window: local morning (7–10 AM PHT) lines up with US evening leisure hours (7–10 PM EST)

The snowball effect

Low early views are normal — it takes time for the system to learn what a channel makes and who wants to see it. Growth tends to arrive in a jump rather than a straight line: one video breaks through, and the algorithm cycles those new viewers back through the rest of the catalog.

04 — YouTube

The YouTube Automation Blueprint

Building a faceless channel end to end — setup, niche, AI-assisted production, upload optimization, growth, and eventually outsourcing the whole workflow.

The premise: run a channel where scripting, voiceover, editing, and thumbnails are delegated or AI-assisted, without appearing on camera. Automation here doesn't mean no work — it means using tools and systems to produce at a pace a single person filming themselves couldn't match.

Chapter 1 — Setting up the channel

1

Use a trusted Google account

An account with existing watch history and activity reads as a real person, not a fresh burner.

2

Create a brand channel

Rather than a personal one — allows adding editors later and keeps it separate from a personal account.

3

Complete the profile

A clean picture, a banner that represents the niche, and a keyword-rich description with any relevant links.

4

Verify with a phone number

Unlocks custom thumbnails, longer videos, and higher daily upload limits — skipping it caps the channel's credibility.

5

Watch, like, comment first

Engaging like a normal viewer before uploading anything establishes the account as active and human.

6

Avoid VPNs / constant IP changes

Frequent location spoofing on a new channel reads as suspicious; stick to one stable, residential connection.

7

Start slow

1–2 videos a day, consistent for 1–2 weeks, on one topic for at least 30–60 days before scaling up.

Chapter 2 — Choosing a profitable niche

The core mistake new automation channels make is posting about everything. A good niche clears three bars: high demand (people actively search it, viral videos already exist in it), evergreen potential (50+ video ideas, stays relevant over time), and monetization potential (ad revenue, affiliate, sponsorship room, with other creators already earning in it).

NicheWhy it works
MotivationEvergreen, highly shareable
Celebrity & drama recapsAlways trending
Personal financeHigh ad rates
Gaming highlightsViral potential, fast-paced
Top 10 / factsEasy to script and produce at volume
Health & fitnessAffiliate-friendly, repeatable
Niches to avoid without experience

Kids' content (heavily restricted, hard to monetize), controversial or political topics (demonetization risk), reposted TV/film content (copyright risk), and oversaturated general categories like plain vlogs.

Chapter 3 — Sourcing & creating content

Idea generation leans on the platform's own search autocomplete, the trending tab, and a simple prompt asking a language model for niche-specific short-form ideas. A script follows a fixed three-beat structure: a hook in the first three seconds, the value or story in the middle, and a direct call to action at the end. Voiceover can be recorded or generated with text-to-speech tools; footage comes from royalty-free stock libraries or, where fair use commentary applies, source clips reworked with edits, voiceover, and added context.

Editing basics: cut dead air, add sound effects, insert subtitles (most people watch muted, so subtitles are a major watch-time lever), and keep the aspect ratio vertical for Shorts.

Chapter 4 — Uploading & optimizing

1

Right format

9:16 vertical, under 60 seconds, at least 720p, with a keyword-bearing filename.

2

Scroll-stopping title

Hook + emotion + keyword, capitalized on key words, ideally under 50 characters.

3

Keyword-rich description

The main keyword and a clear call to action in the first two or three lines, since that's what shows in search.

4

Relevant tags

Still useful for topic classification even though their ranking weight has dropped over time.

5

Right settings

Public visibility, correct made-for-kids setting, vertical framing confirmed, and added to a themed playlist.

Chapter 5 — Growing & monetizing

Growth levers: consistency (ideally daily), a strong first three seconds, regularly checking analytics for watch time and retention drop-off, reverse-engineering what's already working in the niche, and replying to early comments to boost engagement.

Monetization paths beyond the standard Partner Program threshold (1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours, or 10M Shorts views in 90 days): affiliate links through networks like Amazon Associates, selling digital products such as ebooks or templates, and direct sponsorships once a video is consistently clearing a few thousand views.

Chapter 6 — Full automation & scaling

Once a channel is producing consistent views, the workflow itself can be delegated: a scriptwriter, an editor, and optionally a voiceover artist, coordinated through a simple free stack (Trello or Notion for tracking, Google Drive for assets, Sheets for performance). Once the system runs smoothly on one channel, it can be duplicated into a second niche or language rather than rebuilt from scratch.

05 — YouTube

Ranking Strategy

A format breakdown for countdown / ranking-style videos — clip selection, hook, order, and retention editing.

Step 1 — Clip selection

Priority goes to before-and-after clips. They create natural suspense — the viewer wants to see the result, so they stay to the end.

Step 2 — The hook

"This is the best [TOPIC] ever made — because number 5 literally shocked me."

It works in two parts: "this is the best" grabs attention immediately, and calling out that a specific rank shocked the narrator builds instant curiosity — giving the viewer a reason to stay before the video has even really started.

Step 3 — The ranking order

Order runs 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1. The strongest clips open at 5, 4, and 3 to keep people watching early; the weakest clip sits at 2 but stays short so retention doesn't dip; number 1 is the single most satisfying clip, saved to close the video on a real payoff. Satisfaction is the priority — give a genuine ending, not a tease.

Step 4 — Drive comments, not bait

Instead of comment-bait, prompt a genuine reaction through the voiceover or text overlay — something like asking which one was the viewer's favorite. The goal is involvement, not a trick: a viewer who comments is one who stayed satisfied.

Step 5 — Retention editing

  • Keep individual clips to 5–6 seconds max
  • For before/after clips, show the result and cut almost immediately — it creates a natural urge to rewatch
  • If a clip has no before/after arc, keep it short and fill the gap with commentary
  • Keep the rank number clearly on-screen at all times — confusion is what makes people swipe away

Mastering the niche

  1. The curiosity gap: the first three seconds decide everything — a strong verbal hook forces the viewer to stay to see if the payoff is real.
  2. Double down: once a topic is proven, turn it into a numbered series so the audience comes back knowing this account is the go-to for that content.
  3. Visual clarity is non-negotiable: if the current rank isn't obvious at a glance, viewers swipe away.
  4. Beat the mid-video drop: a secondary teaser midway ("number 1 is still coming") keeps tension alive through the middle.
  5. End strong, every time: the closing clip should be the most rewarding moment in the video — that's what drives shares and rewatches.

The safe workflow — follow this exactly

Do not skip or rearrange

The sequence exists for a reason — each phase uses a different tool, and mixing them up is what gets accounts banned.

Phase 1 — Research (NordVPN)

  • Connect to a US NordVPN server
  • Browse trending content and note what's performing well
  • Save ideas and download clips worth editing
  • Disconnect the VPN completely when done
  • Wait 5 full minutes before moving to the next phase

Phase 2 — Content creation (no proxy or VPN)

  • Edit clips completely offline
  • Add captions, music, and effects
  • Prepare all posts and queue everything up
  • Don't open any social apps during this phase

Phase 3 — Upload (iProxy only)

  • Connect through iProxy
  • Open the app and log in — act natural, don't rush
  • Scroll the feed for 2–3 minutes first
  • Upload the content
  • Engage naturally — like, comment, respond — for 15–30 minutes
  • Log out properly when done

Platform-specific safety rules

TikTok — highest risk

Before using a proxy: clear the TikTok cache completely, log out for a minimum of 24 hours, and remove and reinstall the app fresh.

Safe practices: only ever use iProxy, never a VPN, for posting; one account per proxy, no exceptions; never switch locations on the same account; post at consistent times daily; cap it at 3 posts a day.

YouTube — medium risk

Before using a VPN: use a completely different browser or profile, clear the YouTube watch history, and sign out of the Google account first.

Safe practices: VPN is fine for watching and research only; always upload on the normal connection, no proxy or VPN; never change the country setting on the account; keep the upload location consistent.

Instagram — medium risk

Before using a proxy: clear all Instagram app data, remove any linked accounts from the profile, and turn off activity status.

Safe practices: use iProxy for all posting activity; no automation tools of any kind; all engagement done manually.

Critical warnings — never do these

Instant red flags
  • Never use a VPN and a proxy at the same time — an instant red flag on every platform
  • Never switch countries daily — frequent apparent-location changes trigger restriction algorithms
  • Never log into multiple accounts from the same IP — every account on that IP gets banned together
  • Never use free or cheap proxies — those IPs are usually already blacklisted
  • Never post immediately after setup — jumping straight to posting looks unnatural and flags the account

Always do these

  • Wait a full 24 hours after setup before the first post
  • Act human — scroll, like, and watch content before uploading
  • Build slowly — start with one post a day before scaling
  • Keep a completely consistent posting schedule — same times every day
  • Document everything with screenshots — it saves accounts later

Troubleshooting guide

"Suspicious activity" warning

Stop all activity on the account immediately, wait 48–72 hours without touching it, return with completely normal browsing behavior, and don't change any settings — leave everything exactly as it was.

Low or zero views

Check for a shadowban using a different account to search for the content, verify the iProxy connection is active and working, double-check the posting time against the target audience's peak hours, and honestly evaluate whether the content itself needs work.

Can't log in

Don't try logging in repeatedly — it makes things worse. Clear the app data completely, wait a full 24 hours, and attempt the next login with iProxy already connected.
